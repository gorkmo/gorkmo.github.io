# Developer Statement

I am Garrick Springer, an aspiring game designer with interest in programming and scoring. I like using the medium of video games both to tell stories and to observe the emergent behaviors that arise in games, whether between systems within the game or between systems and players outside the game. Through these motives, I want to create a whole that is better than the sum of its parts, a game that might be small in terms of its data size but has hours of discovery and a high replayability factor that will keep players satisfied for a long time.

For me, games start with a mechanic or a story. From one of those, I develop the other, and then work forward from that pair to flesh it out into a full idea. Along the way, my general style of lightheartedness with a few quirks works its way into the structure, and with a little development I can finish products like The Colorful Contraption, a marble guiding game with an odd control setup based on color, or The Pea Shooter, a first-person shooter about feeding the starving patrons of a restaurant. My other projects tend to come from the desire to prove to myself that I have a good understanding of something by creating it with code, but these are more code-centric than games. I work mostly in the Unity engine, coding with VSCode as an IDE, Reaper as a DAW, Blender as a 3D modeling program, and Photoshop or PaintNET as an image editor.

Someday, I want to make something I can look back at and call my ideal game, something I would not have been able to resist playing at some point in my life. I want to find an audience of people like me who find a quick wit and the satisfaction of understanding something you know not everybody spots right away to be important experiences in media and learn how to design for them. And last, but certainly not least, I aim to enjoy the projects along the way.
